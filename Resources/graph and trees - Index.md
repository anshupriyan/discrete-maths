# Resource Extract: Graphs and Trees (graph and trees.pdf)

This index note provides context and quick links for the PDF located in this directory: [[graph and trees.pdf]].

---

## 📖 Resource Overview

This PDF is the primary reading reference for **Chapter 05: Graphs and Trees** in your Discrete Mathematics syllabus. It contains textbook pages covering graph terminology, representations, isomorphism, connectivity, Euler/Hamilton paths, planar graphs, graph coloring, and trees.

---

## 🗺️ Mapping of Study Notes

Below is the structured roadmap of study notes mapped to the sections in this PDF resource:

### 🟩 1. Graph Fundamentals & Connectivity
* **Pages 1–4 (Section 10.1):** [[01. Introduction to Graphs]] - Graph models, vertex degree, Handshaking Lemma, and isolated/pendant vertices.
* **Pages 5–9 (Section 10.2):** [[01. Introduction to Graphs]] - Special families of simple graphs ($K_n, C_n, W_n, Q_n$), bipartite graphs, complete bipartite graphs ($K_{m,n}$).
* **Pages 10–16 (Section 10.3):** [[02. Graph Representations and Isomorphism]] - Adjacency lists, adjacency matrices, incidence matrices, and graph isomorphism (excluding algorithms and applications of isomorphism).
* **Pages 17–25 (Section 10.4):** [[03. Connectivity and Paths]] - Paths, circuits, connected components, strongly/weakly connected digraphs, cut vertices, bridges, path-isomorphism invariants, and path counting using $A^r$.

### 🟦 2. Advanced Graph Topics
* **Pages 27–34 (Section 10.5):** [[04. Euler and Hamilton Paths]] - Euler circuits/paths, Hamilton circuits/paths, Dirac's Theorem, Ore's Theorem, and applications (TSP, Gray codes).
* **Pages 35–36 (Section 10.6):** [[05. Shortest-Path Problems and Dijkstra]] - Shortest-path problems on weighted graphs and Dijkstra's algorithm.
* **Pages 37–42 (Section 10.7):** [[06. Planar Graphs]] - Planar representation, regions, Euler's formula, nonplanarity proofs ($K_5, K_{3,3}$), and Kuratowski's theorem (excluding Section 10.8 Graph Coloring).

### 🟨 3. Trees
* **Pages 43–46 (Section 11.1.1):** [[07. Introduction to Trees and Properties]] - Definition of trees/forests, unique path theorem (excluding Section 11.1.2 Rooted Trees).
* **Pages 47–48 (Section 11.1.3):** [[07. Introduction to Trees and Properties]] - Theorem 2 ($E = V - 1$ with mathematical induction proof) only. (All other properties in 11.1.3 and Sections 11.2 - 11.4 Spanning Trees/Algorithms are excluded from the syllabus).
