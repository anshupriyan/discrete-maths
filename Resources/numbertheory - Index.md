# Resource Extract: Number Theory

This index note provides context and quick links for the PDF located in this directory: [[numbertheory.pdf]].

## 📖 Resource Overview

This PDF serves as the core reading reference for **Chapter 03: Number Theory** in your Discrete Mathematics syllabus. It covers the properties of integers, prime factorization, modular arithmetic, and advanced modular theorems.

---

## 🗺️ Mapping of Study Notes

Below is the structured roadmap that we will build and link to this PDF resource:

### 🟩 1. Divisibility and Primes
* [[01. Divisibility and the Division Algorithm]] - Divisibility definitions, divisibility properties, and the Division Algorithm.
* [[02. Prime Numbers and Factorization]] - Prime vs. composite numbers, Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic (unique factorization), and trial division.
* [[03. The Infinitude of Primes]] - Euclid's proof of the infinitude of primes, and Mersenne primes.
* [[04. Greatest Common Divisor and Least Common Multiple]] - GCD and LCM definition, properties, and relatively prime integers.
* [[05. The Euclidean Algorithm]] - Theoretical basis and step-by-step division steps of the Euclidean algorithm.

### 🟦 2. Modular Arithmetic
* [[06. Introduction to Modular Arithmetic]] - Modular congruences, equivalence classes, and arithmetic operations modulo $m$.
* [[07. Applications of Modular Arithmetic]] - Hash functions, pseudorandom number generators (LCG), and check digits (ISBN, UPC).

### 🟨 3. Advanced Number Theory & Theorems
* [[08. Bezouts Identity and the Extended Euclidean Algorithm]] - Bézout's Identity, Bézout coefficients, and the Extended Euclidean Algorithm.
* [[09. Modular Inverses]] - Multiplicative modular inverse definitions and solving via the Extended Euclidean Algorithm.
* [[10. Linear Congruences]] - Solving single-variable linear congruences $ax \equiv b \pmod m$.
* [[11. The Chinese Remainder Theorem]] - Solving simultaneous linear congruences with pairwise coprime moduli.
* [[12. Fermats Little Theorem]] - Fermat's Little Theorem and fast modular exponentiation.
* [[13. Eulers Totient Function and Eulers Generalization]] - Euler's totient function $\phi(n)$ and the Euler generalization.

### 🟪 4. Integer Representations and Computer Arithmetic
* [[14. Integer Representations and Base Conversions]] - Positional base expansions (binary, octal, hexadecimal), conversions to decimal, and the base expansion division algorithm.
* [[15. Integer Algorithms and Modular Exponentiation]] - Bitwise operations (addition, multiplication), programmatic div/mod, and the repeated squaring method for fast modular exponentiation.
