# Kenneth Rosen: Discrete Mathematics and its Applications (8th Edition)

This index note provides context and quick links for the textbook PDF file located in this directory: [[RosenDiscreteMath8Ed.pdf]].

---

## 📖 Textbook Overview

Kenneth H. Rosen's *Discrete Mathematics and its Applications* is the definitive global standard for introductory and intermediate discrete mathematics courses. It bridges the gap between pure mathematical theory and practical computer science applications.

### Key Focus Areas:
1. **Mathematical Logic & Proofs**: Foundation of computer science logic.
2. **Basic Structures**: Sets, functions, sequences, sums, and matrices.
3. **Number Theory & Cryptography**: Essential algorithms (modular arithmetic, primes, RSA).
4. **Induction & Recursion**: Inductive proofs and recursive definitions.
5. **Counting (Combinatorics)**: Permutations, combinations, Pigeonhole Principle, and binomial coefficients.
6. **Relations**: Equivalence relations and partial orderings.
7. **Graphs & Trees**: Network modeling, paths, isomorphism, and minimum spanning trees.

## 🗺️ Mapping of Created Study Notes

Below is the mapping of your created sequentially structured study notes corresponding to sections in this textbook:

### Chapter 1: Basic Structures — Sets
* **Section 2.1.1**: [[01. Introduction to Sets]] - Core definitions, set descriptions, and number sets.
* **Sections 2.1.2 - 2.1.4**: [[02. Venn Diagrams, Subsets, and Cardinality]] - Set visualization, subsets, and cardinality.
* **Section 2.1.5**: [[03. Power Sets]] - Finite power sets, empty set subsets, and the cardinality theorem.
* **Section 2.1.6**: [[04. Cartesian Products]] - Ordered pairs, n-tuples, and coordinate sets.
* **Sections 2.2 - 2.2.1**: [[05. Set Operations]] - Unions, intersections, set differences, and complements.
* **Section 2.2.2**: [[06. Proving Set Identities]] - Proof methods (containment, logical equivalence, membership tables).
* **Section 2.2.3**: [[07. Generalized Unions and Intersections]] - Finite and infinite indexed collections.
* **Section 2.2.4**: [[08. Computer Representation of Sets]] - Bit string representation of sets and fast bitwise operations.
* **Section 2.2.5**: [[09. Multisets]] - Unordered collections with repetitive elements, multiplicity, operations, and sum rule.

### Chapter 2: Basic Structures — Functions
* **Sections 2.3 - 2.3.1**: [[10. Introduction to Functions]] - Domains, codomains, preimages, ranges, images, and arithmetic operations on functions.
* **Section 2.3.2**: [[11. One-to-One and Onto Functions]] - Injective, surjective, and bijective mappings, proofs and classifications.
* **Section 2.3.3**: [[12. Inverse Functions and Composition]] - Inverse functions, composition of functions, identity, and domain restriction.
* **Section 2.3.4**: [[13. The Graphs of Functions]] - Geometric visualization of functions, discrete/continuous coordinate plotting, and the vertical line test.
* **Section 2.3.5**: [[14. Floor and Ceiling Functions]] - Rounding functions (floor/ceiling), staircase step graphs, and data transmission/algebraic proofs.
* **Section 2.3.6**: [[15. Partial Functions]] - Abstract mathematical partial mappings, domains of definition, and handling computational undefined states.

### Chapter 3: Basic Structures — Sequences and Summations
* **Section 2.4.1**: [[16. Introduction to Sequences]] - Sequences as function mappings, subscripts term notations, and domain index ranges.
* **Section 2.4.2**: [[17. Geometric and Arithmetic Progressions]] - Constant ratio (geometric) and constant difference (arithmetic) progressions and linear/exponential analogies.
* **Section 2.4.5**: [[18. Summations]] - Sigma notation, indexing boundaries, index shifting, closed-form summation shortcuts, and double summations.

### Chapter 8: Advanced Counting Techniques
* **Section 8.5**: [[19. The Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion]] - The Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion (PIE) for 2, 3, and n sets, dynamic Venn diagram justifications, and outside-elements counting.

### Chapter 9: Relations
* **Sections 9.1 - 9.1.1**: [[20. Introduction to Relations]] - Foundational binary relations, relations vs. functions, and relations on a single set.
* **Section 9.1.2**: [[21. Functions as Relations]] - Evaluating functions as highly restricted relations, coordinate graphs, and the Function Condition.
* **Section 9.1.3**: [[22. Relations on a Set]] - Relations within a single set, directed graphs (digraphs) visualization, loops, and counting relations ($2^{n^2}$).
* **Section 9.1.4**: [[23. Properties of Relations]] - Properties of relations (reflexive, symmetric, antisymmetric, transitive), logical proofs, and graphical checks.
* **Section 9.1.5**: [[24. Combining Relations]] - Combining relations via set operations (union, intersection, difference), sequential compositions ($S \circ R$), and relational powers ($R^n$).
* **Sections 9.3 - 9.3.2**: [[25. Representing Relations]] - Representing relations on finite sets using zero-one matrices and evaluating properties through matrix symmetry and diagonals.
* **Section 9.3.3**: [[26. Representing Relations Using Digraphs]] - Visualizing binary relations on finite sets using directed graphs (digraphs) and analyzing relation properties visually.
* **Sections 9.4 - 9.4.4**: [[27. Closures of Relations]] - Introduction to relational closures (reflexive, symmetric, and transitive), paths, and matrix-based path counting.
* **Sections 9.5 - 9.5.2**: [[28. Equivalence Relations]] - Introduction to equivalence relations, properties (reflexivity, symmetry, transitivity), testing relations, and analysis of string length and divisibility examples.
* **Sections 9.6 - 9.6.3**: [[29. Partial Orderings]] - Introduction to partial orderings (posets), properties (reflexivity, antisymmetry, transitivity), divides/greater-than posets, lexicographic ordering, Hasse diagrams, minimal/maximal elements, and visual trace exercises.

### Chapter 10 & 11: Graphs and Trees
* **Sections 10.1 - 10.2**: [[01. Introduction to Graphs]] - Graph models, basic terminology, vertex degrees, Handshaking Lemma, and special families of simple graphs.
* **Section 10.3**: [[02. Graph Representations and Isomorphism]] - Adjacency lists, adjacency matrices, incidence matrices, and graph isomorphism.
* **Section 10.4**: [[03. Connectivity and Paths]] - Paths, circuits, connectedness (undirected/directed), cut vertices, bridges, path-isomorphism invariants, and path counting using $A^r$.
* **Section 10.5**: [[04. Euler and Hamilton Paths]] - Euler paths/circuits, Hamilton paths/circuits, Dirac's and Ore's Theorems, TSP, and Gray codes.
* **Section 10.6.1**: [[05. Shortest-Path Problems and Dijkstra]] - Shortest-path problems on weighted graphs and Dijkstra's algorithm.
* **Sections 10.7.1 - 10.7.2**: [[06. Planar Graphs]] - Planar graphs, Euler's formula with proofs of corollaries, nonplanarity of $K_5$ and $K_{3,3}$, and Kuratowski's Theorem.
* **Sections 11.1.1 & 11.1.3**: [[07. Introduction to Trees and Properties]] - Introduction to trees/forests, unique path theorem, and the tree edge theorem ($E = V - 1$) with mathematical induction proof.

### Chapter 8: Advanced Counting Techniques
* **Sections 8.1 - 8.2**: [[03. Recurrence Relations and Modeling]], [[04. Solving Homogeneous Linear Recurrences]], and [[05. Solving Non-Homogeneous Linear Recurrences]] - Recurrence relations modeling and solving linear homogeneous/non-homogeneous recurrences.
