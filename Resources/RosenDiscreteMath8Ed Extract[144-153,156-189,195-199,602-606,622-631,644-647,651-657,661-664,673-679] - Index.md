## 🗺️ Mapping of Created Study Notes

Below is the mapping of your created sequentially structured study notes corresponding to the exact page sequences in this extract:

### Unit 1: Sets (Pages 144–189)
* **Pages 144–147**: [[01. Introduction to Sets]] - Core definitions, set descriptions, and standard number sets.
* **Pages 148–151**: [[02. Venn Diagrams, Subsets, and Cardinality]] - Set visualization, subsets, and cardinality.
* **Pages 151–153**: [[03. Power Sets]] - Finite power sets, empty set subsets, and the cardinality theorem.
* **Pages 156–158**: [[04. Cartesian Products]] - Ordered pairs, n-tuples, and coordinate sets.
* **Pages 160–165**: [[05. Set Operations]] - Unions, intersections, set differences, and complements.
* **Pages 166–171**: [[06. Proving Set Identities]] - Proof methods (containment, logical equivalence, membership tables).
* **Pages 171–174**: [[07. Generalized Unions and Intersections]] - Finite and infinite indexed collections.
* **Pages 175–178**: [[08. Computer Representation of Sets]] - Bit string representation of subsets and bitwise computer operations.
* **Pages 179–181**: [[09. Multisets]] - Multiple-membership sets, roster/multiplicity notation, and multiset operations.

### Unit 1: Functions (Pages 195–199)
* **Pages 195–199**: [[10. Introduction to Functions]] - Domains, codomains, preimages, ranges, images, and arithmetic operations.
* **Section 2.3.2**: [[11. One-to-One and Onto Functions]] - Injective, surjective, and bijective classifications and proofs.
* **Section 2.3.3**: [[12. Inverse Functions and Composition]] - Inverse functions, compositions, identity properties, and domain restrictions.
* **Section 2.3.4**: [[13. The Graphs of Functions]] - Geometric visualization of functions, discrete/continuous coordinate plotting, and the vertical line test.
* **Section 2.3.5**: [[14. Floor and Ceiling Functions]] - Rounding functions (floor/ceiling), staircase step graphs, and data transmission/algebraic proofs.
* **Section 2.3.6**: [[15. Partial Functions]] - Abstract mathematical partial mappings, domains of definition, and handling computational undefined states.

### Unit 1: Sequences (Section 2.4)
* **Section 2.4.1**: [[16. Introduction to Sequences]] - Sequences as function mappings, subscripts term notations, and domain index ranges.
* **Section 2.4.2**: [[17. Geometric and Arithmetic Progressions]] - Constant ratio (geometric) and constant difference (arithmetic) progressions and linear/exponential analogies.
* **Section 2.4.5**: [[18. Summations]] - Sigma notation, indexing boundaries, index shifting, closed-form summation shortcuts, and double summations.

### Unit 1: Advanced Counting (Section 8.5)
* **Section 8.5**: [[19. The Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion]] - The Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion (PIE) for 2, 3, and n sets, dynamic Venn diagram justifications, and outside-elements counting.

### Unit 3: Relations (Section 9.1)
* **Sections 9.1 - 9.1.1**: [[20. Introduction to Relations]] - Foundational binary relations, relations vs. functions, and relations on a single set.
* **Section 9.1.2**: [[21. Functions as Relations]] - Evaluating functions as highly restricted relations, coordinate graphs, and the Function Condition.
* **Section 9.1.3**: [[22. Relations on a Set]] - Relations within a single set, directed graphs (digraphs) visualization, loops, and counting relations ($2^{n^2}$).
* **Section 9.1.4**: [[23. Properties of Relations]] - Properties of relations (reflexive, symmetric, antisymmetric, transitive), logical proofs, and graphical checks.
* **Section 9.1.5**: [[24. Combining Relations]] - Combining relations via set operations (union, intersection, difference), sequential compositions ($S \circ R$), and relational powers ($R^n$).
* **Sections 9.3 - 9.3.2**: [[25. Representing Relations]] - Representing relations on finite sets using zero-one matrices and evaluating properties through matrix symmetry and diagonals.
* **Section 9.3.3**: [[26. Representing Relations Using Digraphs]] - Visualizing binary relations on finite sets using directed graphs (digraphs) and analyzing relation properties visually.
